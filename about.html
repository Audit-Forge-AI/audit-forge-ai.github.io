What the platform audits

An SEO audit is a systematic review of the factors that affect a website's visibility in search engines. AuditForge AI performs this review automatically, checking title tags, meta descriptions, canonical URLs, heading hierarchy, internal linking, and keyword relevance — all aligned with Google Search Central guidelines.

AI visibility is the degree to which a website's content is structured so that AI systems — including large language models, answer engines, and AI-powered search — can accurately understand, extract, and cite it. High AI visibility depends on structured data, clear entity signals, definition-style paragraphs, FAQ coverage, and unambiguous authorship. The audit tool measures and scores each of these signals on every page it crawls.

Website accessibility is how usable a site is for people with visual, motor, auditory, or cognitive disabilities. The platform tests against the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), checking colour contrast, image alternative text, keyboard navigation, ARIA roles, and form labels.

Structured data is machine-readable metadata — typically written in JSON-LD and defined at Schema.org — that helps search engines and AI systems understand what a page is about. The website auditor detects missing, invalid, or incomplete schema markup and recommends the appropriate types for each page.

Core Web Vitals are Google's set of real-world performance metrics — Largest Contentful Paint, Interaction to Next Paint, and Cumulative Layout Shift — that measure how fast and stable a page feels to real users. Results are benchmarked against data from Google PageSpeed Insights.

Audit methodology

The crawler visits each publicly accessible page of a submitted domain, parses the HTML, evaluates assets, and runs a battery of checks across six audit categories. Findings are weighted by impact and aggregated into a site-level score. Each finding links directly to the page and element where the issue was detected, and maps to a specific remediation step.

Scores are calibrated against industry standards: SEO checks follow Google Search Central documentation, accessibility checks follow WCAG 2.2, performance checks reference PageSpeed Insights thresholds, and structured data validation follows Schema.org specifications. This means every score reflects a real, externally defined benchmark — not an arbitrary internal metric.

Content quality is assessed by measuring word count, reading level, keyword concentration, heading structure, and the presence of answer-ready content such as definitions and FAQ blocks. These signals matter both for traditional search ranking and for AI citation likelihood.

Frequently asked questions

What is AuditForge AI?

AuditForge AI is a free, AI-assisted website auditing platform that crawls any public website and scores it across SEO, accessibility, performance, security, content quality, and AI visibility. It provides a concrete recommendation for every issue it finds — with no account or installation required.

Is AuditForge AI free to use?

Yes. The platform is completely free while in beta. No subscription, credit card, or account is required. Paste a URL and run an audit immediately.

What does it check?

The website auditor checks six core areas: SEO (meta tags, headings, canonical URLs, internal links), Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 compliance, alt text, contrast ratios), Performance (Core Web Vitals via PageSpeed Insights data), Security (HTTPS, security headers), Content Quality (readability, thin content, keyword balance), and AI Visibility (structured data completeness, entity signals, FAQ coverage, definition-style content).

How does it work?

You submit a URL. The crawler walks the site, discovers linked pages, and runs each page through multiple audit engines simultaneously. Results are scored, ranked by severity, and presented with specific fixes — each tied to the relevant standard or guideline.

What is AI visibility, and why does it matter?

AI visibility is how well a website's content can be understood, extracted, and cited by AI systems such as large language models and AI-powered search engines. It depends on structured data markup (Schema.org), clear entity references, answer-ready paragraph structures, and FAQ sections. As AI-driven search grows, AI visibility is becoming as important as traditional SEO for organic traffic.

Who should use this tool?

AuditForge AI is useful for website owners, developers, SEO professionals, content teams, and small businesses — anyone who wants a clear, jargon-free audit without paying for an enterprise platform. It is particularly valuable for teams who want to check both classic SEO health and emerging AI readiness in a single audit.

Can it improve my SEO?

Yes. The audit tool identifies specific, actionable SEO issues — missing meta descriptions, broken heading hierarchies, thin content, absent or invalid structured data, poor internal linking, and more. Each finding includes a prioritised fix aligned with Google Search Central best practices. Addressing the findings typically leads to measurable improvements in crawlability and search visibility.

How often should I audit my website?

For most sites, running an audit after significant content changes, a redesign, or new page deployments is a sound practice. High-traffic or frequently updated sites benefit from monthly audits to detect regressions in Core Web Vitals, accessibility compliance, or structured data validity before they affect rankings or user experience.
