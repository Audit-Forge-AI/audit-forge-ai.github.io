What the platform audits

An SEO audit is a systematic review of the factors that affect a website's visibility in search engines. AuditForge AI performs this review automatically, checking title tags, meta descriptions, canonical URLs, heading hierarchy, internal linking, and keyword relevance — all aligned with Google Search Central guidelines.

AI visibility is the degree to which a website's content is structured so that AI systems — including large language models, answer engines, and AI-powered search — can accurately understand, extract, and cite it. High AI visibility depends on structured data, clear entity signals, definition-style paragraphs, FAQ coverage, and unambiguous authorship. The audit tool measures and scores each of these signals on every page it crawls.

Website accessibility is how usable a site is for people with visual, motor, auditory, or cognitive disabilities. The platform tests against the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), checking colour contrast, image alternative text, keyboard navigation, ARIA roles, and form labels.

Structured data is machine-readable metadata — typically written in JSON-LD and defined at Schema.org — that helps search engines and AI systems understand what a page is about. The website auditor detects missing, invalid, or incomplete schema markup and recommends the appropriate types for each page.

Core Web Vitals are Google's set of real-world performance metrics — Largest Contentful Paint, Interaction to Next Paint, and Cumulative Layout Shift — that measure how fast and stable a page feels to real users. Results are benchmarked against data from Google PageSpeed Insights.

Audit methodology

The crawler visits each publicly accessible page of a submitted domain, parses the HTML, evaluates assets, and runs a battery of checks across six audit categories. Findings are weighted by impact and aggregated into a site-level score. Each finding links directly to the page and element where the issue was detected, and maps to a specific remediation step.

Scores are calibrated against industry standards: SEO checks follow Google Search Central documentation, accessibility checks follow WCAG 2.2, performance checks reference PageSpeed Insights thresholds, and structured data validation follows Schema.org specifications. This means every score reflects a real, externally defined benchmark — not an arbitrary internal metric.

Content quality is assessed by measuring word count, reading level, keyword concentration, heading structure, and the presence of answer-ready content such as definitions and FAQ blocks. These signals matter both for traditional search ranking and for AI citation likelihood.

Who uses AuditForge AI

The audit tool is designed for anyone who publishes on the web and wants an honest, technical picture of how their site performs. In practice, that includes:

Website owners and small businesses

Owners who want to understand why their site isn't ranking or converting, without decoding a spreadsheet of raw Lighthouse data. The platform translates technical findings into plain-language fixes with clear priority levels.

Developers and engineers

Developers who need a fast, automated baseline before launching a new site or after a major refactor. The crawler surfaces regressions in structured data, heading hierarchy, or Core Web Vitals that are easy to miss in code review.

SEO professionals and content teams

Practitioners who want to cross-check their work against an independent tool that covers both traditional SEO signals and emerging AI visibility factors — in a single pass, without stitching together five separate tools.

Why it's free: The platform is an independent open project. The goal is to make professional-grade auditing accessible to sites that can't afford enterprise crawlers, and to learn what signals matter most for AI visibility as that landscape evolves.

Frequently asked questions

Who created AuditForge AI?

AuditForge AI was created by Sourav Bagchi as an independent open project. It started as a personal tool to diagnose why websites were underperforming in search and evolved into a full auditing platform covering SEO, accessibility, performance, security, content quality, and AI visibility.

Why was AuditForge AI built?

Most professional audit tools are expensive, opaque about their methodology, or require agency contracts to access. The platform was built to fill that gap — providing the same depth of analysis available in enterprise crawlers, presented transparently and without a paywall. A secondary motivation was tracking the emerging importance of AI visibility as AI-powered search engines begin to reshape organic traffic.

Why is it free?

The platform is free because the goal is access, not revenue. Small businesses, independent creators, and developers building side projects should not have to pay hundreds of dollars a month to find out if their site has a broken heading hierarchy or missing structured data. While the tool remains in beta, all audits are unlimited and free.

Is AuditForge AI open source?

AuditForge AI is built in the open as an independent project with a focus on transparency. The audit methodology, scoring weights, and the standards each check references are documented so users understand exactly what a score means and why a finding was flagged — rather than receiving a black-box number with no explanation.

How are audit scores calculated?

Each page is evaluated across six audit categories. Within each category, individual checks are weighted by their potential impact on search visibility, user experience, or AI citation likelihood. Weighted findings are aggregated into a category score, and category scores are combined into a site-level score. Every finding links to the specific page element and the external standard it violates, so there are no arbitrary deductions — every point lost traces to a documented rule.

Which standards and guidelines does the platform follow?

Audit checks are mapped to published, externally maintained standards rather than internal rules:

  • SEO — Google Search Central documentation and best practices
  • Accessibility — W3C WCAG 2.2 success criteria
  • Performance — Core Web Vitals thresholds from Google PageSpeed Insights
  • Structured data — Schema types and properties defined at Schema.org

This means every score is reproducible and auditable by the user against the same public sources.

Will AuditForge AI remain free after beta?

The intention is to keep a meaningful free tier permanently. Core auditing — SEO, accessibility, performance, and structured data checks — will remain free. If paid features are introduced in future, they will extend the platform rather than gate what is currently available. No decision on pricing has been made, and no timeline for leaving beta has been set.

How can I contribute feedback or report an issue?

Feedback is the most valuable input the project receives during beta. If an audit produces an incorrect finding, misses a real issue, or a score seems miscalibrated, reporting it directly improves accuracy for everyone. The best way to reach the team is through the contact page. Bug reports, methodology questions, and feature suggestions are all welcome.
